What is an Introvert?

An Introvert tends to have a lot of different traits to an Extrovert and in most cases are much different than an Extrovert

Introverts tend to need a quiet environment to concentrate and in general like these quite environments when they are alone. Introverts also tend to be very reflective and self aware on things they do. Introverts usually think before they speak. If Introverts have a chance to work alone or in a group for the most part they would choose to work alone. Introverts also tend to have a few close friends, than many friends.

There are many different types of introverts such as Social Introverts who like small groups, Thinking Introverts who have a lot of imagination, Anxious Introverts who are mainly introverted because they feel shy and restrained introverts who think before they act

What is an Extrovert?

An Extrovert is almost on the opposite side of the spectrum compared to an Introvert. An Extrovert feels more comfortable being social and likes big crowds and speaking in big group conversations. Extroverts are much more outgoing and comfortable speaking with new people. However because of them being outgoing they can easily get distracted while doing work. Extroverts also tend to be optimistic and sometimes risk takers. Extroverts tend to like having many friends and dislike alone time. Extroverts are also fine with expressing their own problems with people while introverts might not want to

Am I an Introvert or Extrovert?

Personally I believe I have many traits from both an Introvert and Extrovert but I believe I am slightly more of an Extrovert

I have many traits similar to an Extrovert, I would consider myself outgoing and in general being with groups of my friends and people. I would also consider myself very optimistic.

The reasons I think I also have traits for Introvert is because I don’t like sharing my problems with others and like to keep it to myself. I also don’t dislike alone time like some extroverts do, but I much prefer being with people than alone.

The Results of a Introvert/Extrovert test also said I am 68% Extroverted which makes sense to me as I think there is parts of me that has Introverted traits.

My Myers-Briggs Personality Type

The Personality type I got through the Myers-Briggs Personality Type test is ESTJ-A. People with this personality type are extroverted and also dedicated, which applies to me. people with this trait also tend to be honest which would be a good trait for many careers. Some Careers that fit this personality type include jobs relating to finance such as office manager and real estate agent. There are also some careers such as Mechanical Engineer, Civil Engineer and Physician which are careers that fit this personality type. Personally the only careers of the ones I mentioned I could see me in are Mechanical engineering and Civil Engineering as in general I like creating and designing. In General however, I do agree that other people with my trait could be in these types of careers since most of these careers are in business. It is also important to be honest in business, you do not want to lie to a client as that can ruin repitation.

Personality Strengths and Weaknesses

My Personality ESTJ-A has many different Strengths. A Strength my personality trait has is being dedicated. This means the person is highly devoted to goals they set for themselves and they have the dedication to finish them. They will spend the time and effort to finish these goals, even if they are team-based or individual. People with this trait are also Honest. They won’t lie to you and instead tell the truth even though sometimes it might be too brutally honest. People with my trait are also strong-willed and defend the ideas they might have. It’s very difficult for them to switch ideas. Along with those three main strengths, people with my personality trait also tend to be Loyal, Organized and Optimistic.

However, My Personality ESTJ-A also has a few different weaknesses. People with my personality trait tend to be very stubborn. This means that even if a better idea is brought up, it might be hard for a person like me or a person with my trait to change decisions even if it means changing to a better idea. People with my trait are also judgemental. We tend to dismiss that there can be multiple correct answers but instead, we sometimes criticize if someone has a different opinion to ours. People with my trait also have issues expressing emotion. This can be both positive and negative emotion. For example, if we have something wrong going on in our lives it might be hard to open up about their challenges. On the other side, it might be difficult to express emotion when someone else does a task that would make you happy. Along with these three main weaknesses, people with my trait can also have weaknesses such as caring too much about their social status, having a hard time relaxing and having difficulty being in uncomfortable situations.

For the most part, Some of these Strengths such as being dedicated, honest and Optimistic apply to me. There are also many strengths that can apply to me such as being too judgemental, stubborn and difficult to express emotions when it's negative at times.

Why is Personality important in the workplace?

Personality is very important in the workplace. If you understand your co-worker's personality types, you will be able to collaborate with each other since you can work off their weaknesses and vice versa. Personality can also affect work performance. There are several types of Personalities, whether from the big 5 or Myers-Briggs personality traits that work more efficiently than others. It is important to know what personalities your co-workers have as it can help you know their work style when collaborating. As a manager or supervisor, It is important to know your employee's personality types. If you want to put your employees in groups to collaborate, you don’t want to put 5 people with the same personality in a group, or 5 people who are all leaders in a group, it is best to split them up. If you know your employee's personalities, you can get to know them better and the manager can work differently with employees with different personality types.
